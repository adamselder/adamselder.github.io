Chapter 17 Modeling: Durations

One of the key features of a dynamic partnership network is the inclusion of partnership-specific duration. In our analysis we include two partnership types that have a duration associated with them, the cohab partnership network and the persistent partnership network. For both networks we model a homogeneous dissolution process, or more precisely, there is a constant hazard or relationship persistence. In the STERGM framework this takes the simple form of an edges only model for dissolution. This approach has several advantages which have been described elsewhere (cite Pavel) but most importantly for our purposes it assumes a geometric distribution of relationship duration which in turn allows us to use egocentric reports of the mean age of extant ties to determine the probability of tie persistence, because in this unique case the bias from right censoring is exactly canceled out by bias towards selecting longer duration partnerships in cross-sectional data collection. In many previous studies, most of which have focused on MSM, this approach has been sufficient to capture the empirically observed distribution of partnership age which are characterized by large numbers of relationships of short duration and a long right tail. However, we found that in this context the geometric distribution assumption was problematic. The heterosexual relationships we observed in the NSFG did not closely follow a geometric distribution and the deviation from this form was most pronounced among cohabiting partnerships. Figure XX provides the empirical and simulated distribution of persistent and cohab extant ties. In both cases the simulated results based on the geometric distribution shown in blue puts far too much density on the short durations at the left of the distribution and does not capture the greater density of relationships that are longer than 5-7 years.
